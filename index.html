Jubilee Farm Holiday Cottages

Welcome to Jubilee Farm Holiday Cottages the home of David and Elaine Richardson who have lived in the beautiful Lincolnshire village of Thorpe-on-the-Hill for many years, enjoying the view from Jubilee Farm, of the magnificent Lincoln Cathedral resting in its prominent position above the City of Lincoln, with its many areas of interest and tourist attractions.

Upon arrival you will receive a warm welcome from the family and any advice reference local places of interest, can be provided.

The holiday homes have all been converted into excellent living accommodation and furnished to the very highest standards with ample parking. Everything geared to ensuring your stay is a pleasant and comfortable experience, for all the family.

Whether you’re holidaying in Lincolnshire or visiting family at Lincoln University, Jubilee Farm is the perfect location for your stay. Our aim is to offer quality accommodation at very competitive rates.

The local area

We are located close to the historic city of Lincoln.

Jubilee Farm Cottages are located to the North West of the vast county of Lincolnshire. Within 10 minutes drive of the picturesque City of Lincoln with its magnificent Cathedral and Castle. You can spend many hours wondering around the historic Bailgate area, with its many shops and restaurants, including the popular ‘Wig and Mitre’, and much more. Down hill you can relax around Lincolns ‘Brayford Pool’ at one of its many waterside bars or restaurants, with the Lincoln University positioned over-looking the Marina, home of many river going vessels and barges, or take a cruise along the River Witham to nearby pubs and the David Lloyd Sports Centre, or the trendy ‘Burton Waters’ Shops and Bars, or dine at its exclusive floating Oriental Restaurant. The village of Thorpe-on-the-Hill is also the home of the Lincoln Golf Range and 9 hole course, of which short term membership can be obtained for the duration of your stay.

  • Whisby Nature Reserve

    Whisby Nature Park is a beautiful landscape near Lincoln, full of wildlife, walks and trails. Once barren and lifeless, this amazing place now abounds with wildlife. The park was created by quarrying for sand and gravel, but nature has reclaimed the pits and bare sand, creating habitats for many species. Read more

  • Lincoln Cultural Quarter

    Spanning the east of the city centre and the south entrance to Lincoln's Cathedral Quarter, here you'll find theatre, museums, art, music, cuisine, bars and cafes.
